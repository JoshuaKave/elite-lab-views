California Cyber Innovation Challenge
2019 and 2020

The California Cyber Innovation Challenge is a state competition where school teams come together to compete against eachother in a cybersecurity and forensics competition.

Here are some aspects of the competition

  • Puzzles
  • Puzzles are presented to each team. If solved, the team will gain valuable information, as well as some points
  • An example of a puzzle could be a hexadecimal code as a "condensed password". Next to the code is a drawing of lines connecting words, with black and white dots. In order to find the original password, the team has to realize that the black and white dots correspond to 1's and 0's, convert the hexadecimal to binary, then use the chart to convert the binary into a sentence.
  • Forensics Analysis and Capture the Flag
  • The main part of the compeition is to solve a criminal case. A certain crime has been commited, and it is up to the participants to figure out the culprit. The participants are given suspects' computers, and the participants have to use programs such as Wireshark and Autopsy to uncover suspicious files and activties. Flags are spread across the computer mark progress and award points.
  • After gathering evidence and submitting flags, the top teams with the most points move on to presentations. Each team has to present their findinngs and who they believe the criminal is. Judges decide who wins the competition based on the quality of the presentations.
  • Achievements
  • 2019: I was a part of team Mendenhall, and we won first place.
  • 2020: I was a part of team Amalthea, and we won first place.

Optimist Youth Homes and Family Services Fundraiser
August 2020

My sister and I both play instruments, cello and viola respectively, and we wanted to help the community. We noticed that the OYHFS was asking for instruments, so we started a drive around our neighborhood. We gathered over 40 instruments for the OYHFS, and we also decided to hold a concert in order to raise money. The COVID pandemic prevented us from performing in person, so we created a livestream on Youtube. We advertised to our friends and family, and in the end, we raised over 2300$ for the OYHFS.
